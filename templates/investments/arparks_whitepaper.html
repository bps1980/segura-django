Introduction

The AR Parks App revolutionizes how visitors experience National Parks and cultural sites by offering immersive Augmented Reality (AR) interactions. It blends exploration with digital engagement, enhancing tourism, education, and conservation efforts.

Vision and Mission

Our mission is to bridge nature and technology, providing modern, educational, and interactive experiences for park visitors of all ages while promoting conservation awareness.

Product Overview

  • 3D overlays of landmarks, flora, and fauna
  • Real-time trail and wildlife information
  • Interactive AR maps and guided navigation
  • Educational games for children and students

Features and Use Cases

  • Augmented Historical Tours: Recreate historic events at their original locations.
  • AR Wildlife Spotting: Interactive overlays showcasing animal habitats and conservation facts.
  • Gamified Exploration: Collect AR badges across points of interest.
  • Accessible Mode: Enhanced visual/audio features for disabled visitors.

Technology Stack

  • Frontend: React Native or Flutter
  • AR Engine: Unity3D with Vuforia / ARKit / ARCore
  • Backend: Django with PostgreSQL
  • Mapping: Mapbox or Google Maps API

Development Roadmap

  • Q2 2025: MVP release covering 2 national parks
  • Q3 2025: Expansion to 10 parks, add multilingual support
  • Q4 2025: Launch educational modules and donation tools
  • 2026: Broaden government contracts and global reach

Revenue Model

  • In-app purchases (avatars, upgrades)
  • Government and nonprofit contracts
  • Branded sponsorships (e.g., outdoor brands)
  • Premium subscription for guided tours and educational content

Partnership Opportunities

  • National Park Services
  • AR/VR development firms
  • Environmental nonprofits
  • Tourism boards and educational institutions

Team

Led by Brennen Segura, Segura Mgmt's AR Parks App project combines expertise from developers, AR specialists, and conservation advisors to deliver impactful, tech-forward visitor experiences.
